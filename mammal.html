Mammals

Aquatic mammals that rely on the ocean for their existence. They are an informal group of animals that joined by their reliance on envireonments for their survival.

Bearded Seal
One of the four Arctic seal species that inhabits around Arctic Ocean. Bearded seal is closely related to gray seal and harp seal as it is a member of the "true seal" family.
Beluga Whale
Beluga whale is brown-gray in color at birth and turns white in adulthood. Beluga whale also known for its relatively small tooth. It is one of the only two species of "white whales" family, the other eing narwhal.
Blue Whale
Throughout all of recorded history, the blue whale is the largest creature to ever live on Earth. Its lengths can reach up to 110 feet (33 meters) and weights up to 209 tons (190 tonnes). This immaculous size is possible by their aquatic lifestyles and the buoyancy from seawater.
California Sea Lion
Known for its high intelligence and sosial pinnipeds, California sea lions live in a large pack in coastal waters. They are one of the "eared seals" family and have large flippers that can be used to walk on land.
Orca
One of the most iconic creature in the ocean, the orca is a creature that has one of the largest distribution of species. Orcas live in every ocean from Arctic to Antarctic ocean. This species has been a familiar sight to coastal peoples for thousands of years due to its distinctive dorsal fin and distinctive black and white color pattern.
Polar Bear
Polar bears are by far the largest terrestrial predator in the Arctic, it is also a formidable animal on land as well as in the water. The polar bear may be a subspecies of a brown bear, due to its close relation. however in the Anatomical stand point, there is quite a huge difference as a result of its semi-aquatic lifestyle. One of the biggest adaptation from polar bear is the large feet size, used to walk across thick snow and swimming. Polar bear's weight can go up to over 1500 pounds (700 kg).
Fin Whale
The fin whale is the world's second largest animal throughout the history. Its length is at the very least 85 feet (26 m) and weight up to 80 tons. Fin whale would definitely be crushed by its own weight if it ever lives on land.
Harp Seal
Lives in the Atlantic and Arctic oceans, the harp seal is one of the "true seal". This seal earned its name ad harp seal due to the harp-shaped markings on the back. Migration is a key characteristic of this species, which follows the Arctic sea ice as it expands and contracts throughout the year.
Hourglass Dolphin
Hourglass dolphins are distinguished by black and white markings across their bodies forming a cross-bearing or hourglass pattern across their bodies. The cold waters around Antarctica are ideal for Hourglass dolphins because few other cetaceans swim there. The species can survive in water as cold as -0.3 degrees Celsius, or about 31 degrees Fahrenheit. Researchers rarely misidentify hourglass dolphins due to their icy range and distinctive coloring.
