Shellfish

The term of fisheries for aquatic invertebrates that contains exoskeleton-bearing including various species of crustaceans, echinoderms and mollusks. Most clams are low on the food chain and eat a diet consisting mainly of phytoplankton and zooplankton.

Horseshoe Crab
The horseshoe crab is not a crustacean, however it is more closely related to scorpion and spiders than to shrimps and crabs. This species have been around for 450 million years which making them one of the oldest lineages of animals alive nowadays. The horseshoe crab roamed the seafloor before dinasour existed which is more than 200 million years!
Blue King Crab
In the curstaceans, the blue king crab is one of the largest and . This species worth millions of dollars, unfortunately the numbers have been reduced that fishers no longer target it directly. It usually lives on the soft bottom habitats throughout the Bering Sea. It is decapods along with the true crabs, lobsters and more which they have ten legs.
Queen Conch
The queen conch are large marines snail that found at Caribbean Sea and adjacent waters. They are an important fishery resouces for every country. Having a maximum length of 30cm (one foot) within three to five years. They spend the remainder of their life thickening their shells rather than increasing their sizes.
Atlantic Blue Crab
As far south as Argentina, the Atlantic blue crab (also known as the Chesapeake blue crab) inhabits the east coast of the United States and the Gulf of Mexico. It is named for its blue color and a large number of U.S. crab fisheries target this species outside of Alaska.
American Lobster
The American lobsters (also called Maine lobsters) are large, clawed lobsters that support a wide, profitable fishery throughout their range. Spiny lobsters of the Caribbean and California, as well as other commercially targeted species like the European lobster, are only distantly related to this lobster species.
Caribbean Spiny Lobster
In the Caribbean Sea and nearby waters, the Caribbean spiny lobster is one of the largest crustaceans on coral reefs and seagrass beds, and it is one of the most valuable fishery resources throughout its range. This Caribbean spiny lobster is a decapod and has ten legs. Despite its spiney exoskeleton, which protects it from potential predators, nurse sharks and Nassau groupers are among its favorite prey species.
Chambered Nautilus
The chambered nautilos is the only cephalopods that have external shells and one of the species of nautilus. This species can retract into its shell when it gets threatened like most of the shelled animals.
Geoduck
The geoduck is a largest burrowing clam found at the west coast of North America. It has a small shell which cannot retrack into the shell with the shell below than 20 cm (8 inches), but the soft body can be over approximately 1 m (3.3 feet) long. It can burrow deep into muddy, soft or sandy sediments which it's neck used to bring clean seawater down to the deeply buried shell.
Tiger Prawn
The tiger prawn is a large-bodied prawn that found at Indo-West Pacific Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico. It has been targeted widely by the fisher throughout its range which is a highly valuable seafood prices and it can grown in aquaculture farms all over the world. It is a decapod and it has ten legs which covered with a spiny exoskeleton to provide some protection from potential predators.
