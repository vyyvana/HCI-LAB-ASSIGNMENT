Reptiles

Reptiles are animals that can adapted to aquatic or semiaquatic life in a marine environment.

Banded Sea Krait
The banded sea krait is a kind of sea snake which live on the coral reefs at the eastern Indian and western Pacific oceans. It's name come from the dark, vertical bars (bands) that streak an otherwise white body. Meanwhile, their entire lifecycle in the ocean and they spend more time on land.
Flatback Turtle
The flatback turtle derive their name from the relatively flatness of their shell, one of the characteristics that set them apart from other sea turtles around the world. Throughout their lifetimes, many sea turtles migrate extremely long distances, crossing entire ocean basins numerous times. Nevertheless, the flatback turtle has a smaller range, has never visited the Americas, and is restricted to Papua New Guinea and Australian coastal waters.
Green Turtle
The green turtle is a largest species of the hard-shelled sea's family and second largest of the leatherback turtle's among all sea turtles. It's name comes from it's greenish color of its fat and is the only sea turtle that is a herbivore which diet of seagrass and algae may contribute to the green fatty tissue.
Hawksbill Turtle
The hawksbill turtle's name from the shape of its curved and pointed beak that used to feed on sponges and other invertebrates growing on coral reefs. This turtle spend part of its lives in the open ocean, however it is more reef-associated than other species of sea turtles.
Leatherback Turtle
The leatherback turtle is the largest living turtle which its weight reached up to 2,200 pounds (1,000 kilos). This turtle does not have a hard and bony carapace(shell). Meanwhile, it has a tough and rubbery shell that is composed of cartilage-like tissues.
Loggerhead Turtle
The loggerhead turtle is a broad and strong head sea turtle. Turtles like these are generalist predators and use their powerful jaws and muscular heads to crush hard-shelled invertebrates like queen conch, Caribbean spiny lobster, and Caribbean lobster claws.
Marine Iguana
Marine iguana is the only lizard that spends time in ocean. They can be found at the Galapagos Islands. For a long time, the populations on the islands have been isolated from each other, so they have developed their own species.
Olive Ridley Turtle
The olive ridley turtle is the smallest sea turtle species with only 2 to 2.5 feet (0.6 to 0.7 m) long and 80 to 110 pounds (36 to 50 kg) and also the most abundant sea turtle. It is named olive coloration because of their heart-shaped shells.
Saltwater Crocodile
The saltwater crocodile is the largest reptile and a formidable predator throughout its range. It is not uncommon for saltwater crocodiles of this size to consume just about any animal that escapes from its enclosure and they are particularly adept at drowning terrestrial creatures like birds and mammals. Saltwater crocodiles live in brackish (low salinity) water near the coast since they can survive in full-salinity sea water.
