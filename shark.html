Sharks

Fish that possess cartilaginous skeletons and belong to the elasmobranch family. As a top predator, sharks have played an important role in maintaining healthy oceans for millions of years.

Blacktip Shark
The blacktip shark is a widespread and medium-sized shark which its name comes from its black-tipped pectoral, dorsal and tail fins. It has a torpedo-shaped bodies and are known for spinning out of the water while feeding which often mistaken with spinner shark.
Blue Shark
The blue shark is a curious and open-ocean predators that live throughout the global ocean, from the tropics to cold temperate waters. It spends most of its lives far from the coast and is a pelagic species. The blue color of the skin gives this species its name.
Bull Shark
The bull shark is a predatory species that found in the coastal sea. It has the best ability to move into freshwaters - particularly large, coastal rivers and lakes which is able to move back and forth between saltwater and freshwater with ease. In fact that it has more contact with humans. Therefore they are responsible for fatally biting more people than any other species.
Cownose Ray
The cownose ray is moderately sized stringray that can be founded at western Atlantic ocean. Is is a active swimmers and rarely found lying motionless on the seafloor.
Giant Manta Ray
The giant mantra ray is the largest ray and fish in the world which reaching the widths of up to 29 feet (8.8 m). They have been divided into two species: the giant manta ray, which is a more oceanic species; and the reef manta ray, which is more coastal in nature.
Great Hammerhead Shark
The great hammerhead shark is the largest of the hammerhead species which reaching length of 20 feet (6.1 m) and weight of 991 pounds (450 kg). It has a prominent indentation in the middle.
Great White Shark
The great white shark is the largest predatory fish which its weight can be reached several hundred pounds. The whale shark and the basking shark are the only two fishes that can grow larger than Great Whites.
Spotted Eagle Ray
The spotted eagle ray is one of the largest eagle rays with the widths of nearly 11 feet (over 3 m). It is an active swimmers and do not lie motionless on the seafloor. Besides, it is a foraging predators which known to eat variety of invertebrate and fish prey.
Tiger Shark
The tiger shark is named for the distinctive vertical bars on the sides of their bodies. It is very noticeable in juveniles and at least party visible throughout the lifetime because of its bars fade slightly as individuals reach adulthood. It can reach the length of at least 18 feet (5.5 m) and 2000 pounds (nearly a metric tonne) and known as the fourth largest shark and the second largest predatory shark.
